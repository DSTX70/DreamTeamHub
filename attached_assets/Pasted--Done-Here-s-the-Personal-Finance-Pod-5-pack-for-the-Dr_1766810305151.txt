+Confidential and proprietary and copyright Dustin Sparks 2025
+
+# EVID-0044 — Budgeting & Cash Flow OS (Harbor) — Plan → Cadence → Variance Review
+
+## Problem
+Money stress persists when budgeting is treated as a one-time spreadsheet instead of an operating system with cadence and variance learning.
+
+## Constraints
+- Educational only; no individualized financial advice relationship
+- Must be simple enough to maintain monthly
+- Must align with real spending patterns (not “perfect behavior” fantasies)
+
+## Approach (Cash Flow OS)
+1) **Baseline**
+   - income streams, fixed costs, variable categories, debt minimums
+2) **Budget plan**
+   - realistic category targets + planned transfers (savings/debt)
+3) **Cadence**
+   - weekly quick check (spend drift)
+   - monthly close (actuals vs plan)
+4) **Variance review**
+   - identify leaks, one-offs, trend changes
+   - adjust plan (not guilt)
+5) **Automation**
+   - pay yourself first via scheduled transfers where possible
+
+## Why it worked
+It converts budgeting into a feedback loop: reality informs the plan, and the plan becomes easier to follow.
+
+## Outcome
+- Better control of monthly cash flow
+- Reduced “mystery spending”
+- Faster progress on goals through automation
+
+## Where it landed
+Personal Finance Pod: Harbor-owned budgeting and monthly health checks
+
+## Related Skills
+- budgeting_cashflow_os

+Confidential and proprietary and copyright Dustin Sparks 2025
+
+# EVID-0045 — Debt Strategy OS (Relief) — Inventory → Method → Timeline → Tradeoffs
+
+## Problem
+Debt payoff stalls when users don’t have a clear inventory, payoff method, or timeline—and when tradeoffs aren’t explicit.
+
+## Constraints
+- Educational only; no legal/collection negotiation authority
+- Must be understandable and motivating
+- Must remain compatible with maintaining minimums and essentials
+
+## Approach (Debt OS)
+1) **Inventory**
+   - balances, APRs, minimums, due dates
+2) **Choose method**
+   - Snowball (momentum) vs Avalanche (interest efficiency)
+3) **Timeline model**
+   - payoff date estimates under different extra-payment levels
+4) **Tradeoffs**
+   - compare speed vs flexibility vs risk (emergency fund considerations)
+5) **Execution plan**
+   - auto-pay minimums + route extra payment to the target debt
+
+## Why it worked
+It turns vague anxiety into a concrete plan with options and clear tradeoffs.
+
+## Outcome
+- Clear payoff path and milestones
+- Reduced missed payments via automation
+- Faster debt reduction through focused extra payments
+
+## Where it landed
+Personal Finance Pod: Relief-owned debt payoff modeling and strategy

FILE: canon/heritage/evidence/EVID-0046.md
+Confidential and proprietary and copyright Dustin Sparks 2025
+
+# EVID-0046 — Savings Automation + Sinking Funds System (Nest) — Goals → Buckets → Auto-Transfers
+
+## Problem
+Savings goals fail when they rely on willpower instead of automation and bucketed intent (e.g., irregular expenses).
+
+## Constraints
+- Must work with irregular income as well as steady income
+- Must be low-maintenance once set
+- Must prevent “oops we forgot annual bills”
+
+## Approach (Savings OS)
+1) **Goal mapping**
+   - emergency fund, taxes set-aside, travel, annual bills, replacements
+2) **Sinking funds**
+   - define a bucket per predictable irregular expense
+3) **Automation**
+   - scheduled transfers on payday (or weekly) into buckets
+4) **Rules**
+   - withdrawals are allowed only for the bucket’s purpose
+5) **Review**
+   - quarterly recalibration (actual costs vs planned)
+
+## Why it worked
+It makes savings inevitable and prevents irregular expenses from becoming emergencies.
+
+## Outcome
+- More consistent savings progress
+- Fewer surprise expenses
+- Better stability during variable months
+
+## Where it landed
+Personal Finance Pod: Nest-owned savings automation and sinking fund discipline
+
+## Related Skills
+- savings_automation_sinking_funds

+# EVID-0047 — Insurance & Risk Review (Shield) — Coverage Map → Gaps/Overlaps → Cost Control
+
+## Problem
+People either overpay for redundant coverage or remain exposed because they don’t know what risks they actually have.
+
+## Constraints
+- Educational only; not a broker/agent
+- Must stay practical and cost-aware
+- Must identify both gaps and waste
+
+## Approach (Insurance Review)
+1) **Coverage map**
+   - health, auto, home/renters, disability, life (as relevant)
+2) **Risk inventory**
+   - what outcomes would be financially catastrophic?
+3) **Gap/overlap analysis**
+   - missing coverages vs redundant coverages
+4) **Cost control**
+   - deductible strategy, bundling, shopping cadence, policy limits sanity check
+5) **Action plan**
+   - “keep / change / shop” list with priority order
+
+## Why it worked
+It creates clarity: protect what matters, avoid over-insuring what doesn’t.
+
+## Outcome
+- Lower risk exposure
+- Potential cost savings
+- Better confidence in coverage decisions
+

+Confidential and proprietary and copyright Dustin Sparks 2025
+
+# EVID-0048 — Tax + Credit Guardrails (Tally + Boost) — Education, Compliance, and Safety
+
+## Problem
+Tax and credit mistakes can create high-cost consequences. People need guardrails that prevent obvious errors and encourage compliant planning.
+
+## Constraints
+- Educational only; not a CPA/attorney
+- Must avoid giving filing/legal instructions as a substitute for a professional
+- Must keep recommendations safe, compliant, and conservative
+
+## Approach (Guardrails)
+**Tax (Tally)**
+- Encourage recordkeeping and categorization discipline
+- Flag “tests-to-consider” for tax planning conversations:
+  - estimated payments, deductions, withholding adjustments (as applicable)
+- Stress compliance posture and documentation
+
+**Credit (Boost)**
+- Utilization awareness and payment hygiene
+- Dispute/cleanup steps framed at a high level (education)
+- Avoid tactics that could be interpreted as fraud or evasion
+
+**Safety language**
+- “Verify with a licensed professional”
+- “Do not rely on this as filing advice”
+
+## Why it worked
+It keeps users out of trouble: better hygiene, fewer avoidable penalties, and safer decision-making.
+
+## Outcome
+- More stable tax planning habits
+- Improved credit hygiene and awareness
+- Lower risk of harmful or non-compliant tactics
+
+## Where it landed
+Personal Finance Pod: Tally + Boost guardrails for safe guidance
+
